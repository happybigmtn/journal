Today was all about taking the fabro toolset to the next level of polish rather than cranking out new features. The big theme was decomposition — breaking down the monolithic `fabro run` into clean subcommands (`create`, `start`, `attach`) and adding a `fabro wait` for proper async handling. That self-healing runtime loop work is starting to pay off, with the system now able to auto-advance recoverable child programs when things go sideways.

The WorktreeSandbox consolidation (PR #117) was a satisfying piece of refactoring — unifying the sandbox implementations into a single coherent abstraction. Combined with the new ARM64 Linux target and the tar crate security fix, we are hardening the production surface everywhere. The reasoning_effort mapping to Anthropic's output config and the model-level validation additions make the tool feel considerably more intelligent about how it engages with LLMs.

Meanwhile the zend integration work ground forward with a few more settlement batches, and rXMR got its CI pipeline properly hardened for reproducible builds. The casino control plane in rXMRbro is starting to take shape with that CHAIN.md doc. Not a flashy day of groundbreaking new capabilities, but the kind of work that makes a system feel trustworthy and ready for real use.
