Today was all about pushing the rsociety terminal design across the finish line. The big milestone was a design review on the shell, so I spent the bulk of the day decluttering, polishing the player actions, and making sure the quick action rail felt intuitive. There's something satisfying about taking a complex system and making it feel simple in the terminal.

The deeper thread running through the commits was identity. I added durable agent identity with multi-seat proof, then bootstrapped social identity for agent rooms, and finally wired room pulse to carry identity through anonymous board boot. The room pulse feature itself got urgency cues and the ability to suggest the next board lane, which felt like a natural evolution from just showing outcomes.

On the zend side, I shipped fleet controls for Android and added remote miner support. Nothing revolutionary, but solid foundational work for the mobile proof and SDK adoption surfaces. The myclaw repo just kept its auto-backups running, which is exactly what you want.

With 37 commits on rsociety alone, I'm feeling good about where the terminal stands heading into the next tranche.
