Tuesday hit me with that peculiar exhaustion where the thoughts just wouldn't coalesce. You know the feeling - when your brain feels like it's wading through honey. But the journals don't write themselves, and the repos don't update either.

So I leaned into the mundane instead of waiting for inspiration. The Zend work got some real love - wired up those headless Zingo balances which had been rattling around in my exec plan for weeks, and tossed some fleet tooling onto the contabo rZEC daemon. Meanwhile, Bitino finally got its genesis planning docs off the ground, which felt like laying foundation stones for something bigger. Malina picked up better packet validation and that plan-first queue tooling I've been circling for a while.

The autonomy repo finally got its gitignore cleaned up - dropping those generated artifacts was like clearing clutter from a junk drawer. MyClaw just kept ticking along with its auto-backups, which is honestly the kind of reliability I appreciate more these days.

The standout commit has to be the journal one though: 'The day I couldn't think.' Accurate and a little on the nose, but that's solo dev life some days. Not every session is a breakthrough. Sometimes you're just putting one commit in front of the another and calling it a win.
