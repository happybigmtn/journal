Today was one of those days where the dust settled on a bunch of parallel efforts. The marketing site got some real love -- the new Showcase section went live, the blog and roadmap pages got a long-overdue redesign, and I finally added that tabbed install widget with copy buttons so people can grab the right install method (Claude Code, Codex, or Bash) without friction. Also fixed a few embarrassing typos in the docs that had been bugging me.

On the benchmark front, I recorded the latest runs: Opus 4.6 hit 58.0% on SWE-Bench-Lite (20min timeout, $218.65) and Sonnet 4.6 got 57.7% ($55.22). The gap is narrowing but Opus still leads. I also tuned some concurrency defaults -- dropped patch generation from 150 to 75 workers, then bumped timeout to 20 minutes to give the model more breathing room.

Meanwhile, the Plugin SDK got document CRUD methods added, which opens up a whole new category of integrations. And myosu absorbed a massive amount of spec work -- redesigned the entire interface notation system, filled in gaps across OS.md and design.md, and added detailed specs for TUI implementation and abstraction pipelines.

Overall: feels like I'm building momentum across three fronts -- polishing the product (marketing), measuring progress (benchmarks), and locking in the vision (specs). Things are starting to click.
